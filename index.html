Student Public Service Announcement

Fair Treatment in Hospitality Work

A digital fluency PSA encouraging students to slow down, check the source, and share information responsibly.

  • View the PSA

The issue

Misinformation can move fast through group chats, social feeds, and school communities. A post that looks harmless can confuse people, spread fear, or make it harder to find reliable help.

The action

Before sharing, use three quick checks: who made it, where the evidence comes from, and whether another trustworthy source says the same thing.

The goal

This PSA gives students simple tools for safer online habits, so sharing becomes more thoughtful, useful, and fair to the people who receive it.

Infographic

Check the creator

Look for an author, organisation, or account that can be identified and trusted.

Check the evidence

Look for dates, links, data, and sources that support the claim.

Check another source

Compare the claim with another reliable site before passing it on.

Share responsibly

If it cannot be checked, pause. Sharing nothing is better than spreading harm.

Pause Before You Post

Three checks in thirty seconds.

  1. Who made it?
  2. What evidence is shown?
  3. Who else confirms it?
